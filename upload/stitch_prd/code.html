A developer enters into an oral agreement to purchase a specific parcel of land for $500,000. Before anything is written down, the developer spends $50,000 conducting soil tests and environmental surveys with the seller's knowledge. The seller later refuses to proceed, citing the Statute of Frauds. Analyze the developer's potential remedies based on promissory estoppel and part performance.

Editorial Explanation

This question tests the exceptions to the Statute of Frauds regarding real estate transfers. A complete answer must distinguish between part performance (which typically requires possession and improvements, not just preparatory acts like soil testing) and promissory estoppel (detrimental reliance). Historically, courts are divided on whether mere preparatory acts constitute sufficient reliance to overcome the Statute, leaning heavily on the specific jurisdiction's interpretation of Restatement (Second) of Contracts § 129.
